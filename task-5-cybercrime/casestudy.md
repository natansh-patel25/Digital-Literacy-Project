Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud



UPI payment fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India that targets users of digital payment applications. In this type of fraud, scammers manipulate individuals into approving unauthorized transactions or revealing sensitive information.



A typical scenario begins when a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media. The scammer may pretend to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer in an online transaction. They often create a sense of urgency or trust to convince the victim. The fraudster then sends a "collect request" through a UPI app or asks the user to enter their UPI PIN under the pretext of receiving money.



Unaware of the process, the victim approves the request or enters their PIN, which actually authorizes a payment instead of receiving money. As a result, money is instantly deducted from the victim’s bank account.



This type of fraud usually targets students, elderly individuals, and people who are not fully aware of how digital payment systems work. The consequences include financial loss, mental stress, and reduced trust in online transactions.



This case highlights the importance of digital awareness and cautious behavior while using online payment platforms.
